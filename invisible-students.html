Exposing college student homelessness with a documentary

Client: Berkeley Rent Board
Role: Project Lead / Researcher, Director, Producer
Skills: Storytelling, Qualitative Research

Context: 2017 saw multiple media reports of college homelessness in places like UCLA and Cal State. To reveal the impact of the housing crisis on Berkeley students, Berkeley Rent Stabilization Board charged its interns to collect similar stories on campus. I led the research efforts and the production of a documentary.

Process: By conducting secondary research, we compiled relevant news reports and legislations. Through social media and phone, we then talked to 6 (formerly) homeless Berkeley students. We eventually made a documentary sharing 2 unique perspectives. interview, non-participant observation, and questionnaires. The team identified 5 key populations at risk of food shortage, and mapped them to a beneficiary classification pyramid.

Results: We made these authentic student stories into a documentary and screened it on campus. Since, the film has garnered 5K+ online views, and is currently used as discussion material in Berkeley classrooms.

Through pre-interviews, we identified a common journey of homeless students at UC Berkeley: students who end up homeless are turned away during three critical interfaces with the campus institutions.

Journey mapping

6 interviews revealed similar trajectories towards homelessness despite different student backgrounds, framing the narrative for the film.

A common journey of homeless students at UC Berkeley: students who end up homeless are turned away during three critical interfaces with the campus institution. They do not have on-campus housing; they have maxed out of their financial aid package; they are not aware of the Basic Needs Committee, which would serve as the last defense.

These findings not only shaped the documentary-making process, but also informed the panel discussion at the on-campus screening.

Filming locations were chosen in collaboration with homeless students as a way to achieve mutual trust.
